Region overview

Greater Cairns aircon — the wet-tropics reality.

Near-100% household penetration of split-systems.

Cairns Regional Council census data and energy-utility surveys put household aircon penetration in the Greater Cairns area at very close to 100%. The wet-tropics climate — mean annual humidity 78–82%, mean summer max 32°C, wet-season monsoon Dec through April — makes aircon non-negotiable, not optional. Most homes have at least one split-system in the main living area and a second in the master bedroom; family homes typically run 3–5 head units or a full ducted system.

Two councils, one climate zone.

Cairns Regional Council covers the bulk of the population — Cairns CBD, the northern beaches (Trinity Beach, Kewarra Beach, Palm Cove, Ellis Beach), the inner-west (Mooroobool, Westcourt, Manunda), the southern corridor (Edmonton, White Rock, Bayview Heights), and the western foothills (Redlynch, Brinsmead, Stratford). Douglas Shire Council sits to the north, running from Wangetti through Mossman, Port Douglas, and on to Cape Tribulation. Both fall in the same wet-tropics climate zone — the aircon servicing story is identical for both.

Manufacturer warranty annual-service requirement.

The three brands that cover the bulk of Greater Cairns installations — Daikin, Mitsubishi Electric, and Fujitsu — all require documented annual servicing by a qualified technician for the standard 5-year compressor warranty to remain valid. We’ve had multiple clients come to us in year 3 or 4 with a failed unit and no service records, only to have the warranty claim rejected. Every clean we issue comes with a dated, signed service report carrying our ARCtick licence number — that goes straight in your warranty file.

Tropical mould is the universal issue.

Across every Greater Cairns suburb — coastal, inner-city, hinterland, or Port Douglas — the same two tropical mould species (Cladosporium and Aspergillus) breed on aircon evaporator coils and inside drain pans. They cause the sour-musty smell, release spores into living areas, and slowly degrade refrigeration efficiency as biofilm thickens on the coil. The annual mould treatment is mandatory in this climate — not an upsell.

Coastal vs inland service intervals.

Coastal northern beaches and Port Douglas properties get hit with salt-laden onshore breezes that corrode the outdoor compressor fin pack — those units need 6-monthly service, not annual. Inland and western foothills properties (Redlynch, Brinsmead, Stratford, Mount Sheridan) can run on an annual cycle. Either way, every property benefits from the same five-service catalogue: split-system cleans, ducted service, mould treatment, refrigerant recharge, and the annual service plan.
