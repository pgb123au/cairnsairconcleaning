What we do

The full split-system deep clean.

What we do vs what a filter rinse does.

A filter rinse is what you can do yourself in 5 minutes — pull the plastic mesh, run it under the tap, snap it back in. That’s hygiene, not a service. A proper split-system deep clean is a 60–90 minute job per indoor head where we fully strip the front fascia and barrel fan off the unit, chemically treat the evaporator coil, deep-clean the barrel fan (which carries 60% of the unit’s biofilm and bacterial load), flush the drain pan and condensate line, biocide-spray the drain pan, refit, and pressure-check the refrigerant on a gauge set.

Step-by-step on-site.

  • Wall + floor protection (drop sheets + ceiling cover for outdoor over a deck)
  • Power isolated at the wall and at the isolator on the outdoor unit
  • Front fascia + filter removed and bagged for transport
  • Barrel fan removed (the heavy-bacterial-load component) and chemically cleaned in tray
  • Evaporator coil chemical clean — food-grade coil cleaner via pump sprayer, agitated, rinsed
  • Drain pan and condensate line flushed; biocide spray applied to drain pan
  • Filter washed and re-fitted, or replaced (HEPA option for asthma/allergy households)
  • Anti-microbial fog applied to internal cavity
  • Refit, power on, 15–20 minute cool-down run
  • Gauge set on the outdoor service port: refrigerant pressure check, temperature differential reading
  • Service report with before/after photos, dated, ARCtick licence number recorded

Why this matters in Cairns specifically.

The wet-tropics climate sits at 25–30°C and 70–95% humidity for most of the year. Tropical mould species — Cladosporium and Aspergillus — breed on the evaporator coil and inside the drain pan within months. Southern aircon technicians treating the clean as a dust-and-debris job miss 80% of the actual issue. See our mould treatment page for the tropical-specific anti-microbial protocol we add to every Cairns clean.
